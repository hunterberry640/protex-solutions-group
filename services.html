Should You Repair or Replace Your Roof?

Not every roofing issue calls for a full tear-off. Here's how to tell whether a targeted repair will do the job — or if it's time for a complete replacement.

Signs You Need a Repair

  • Cracked, curled, or absent shingles Individual shingles that are splitting, lifting at the edges, or blown off entirely point to localized wear that a repair crew can address without touching the rest of the roof.
  • Granule buildup in your gutters Finding coarse, sand-like particles in your downspouts means your asphalt shingles are shedding their protective coating — an early warning sign that specific areas need attention.
  • Ceiling stains or interior moisture Discoloration on ceilings or damp patches along walls often trace back to a single compromised flashing or seal. Catching it early keeps a small fix from becoming a big one.

Signs You Need a Replacement

  • Your roof is 20 + years old Most asphalt shingle systems are engineered for a 20–30 year service life. Once your roof enters or passes that window, a full replacement is usually the smarter long-term investment.
  • Visible sagging or uneven planes A roof deck that dips or bows signals structural deterioration beneath the surface — something patching alone can't resolve safely.
  • Widespread damage across 30 %+ of the surface When the affected area covers a third or more of the total roof, the cost of individual repairs typically exceeds the price of a new system — and a replacement delivers far better value.

Not sure which route is right for your property? We'll inspect your roof and give you an honest recommendation — no pressure, no obligation.
